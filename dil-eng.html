Language learning is tedious, but it shouldn't be. Languages are fascinating.

They're all about people of distant lands. Their adventures, customs, and attitudes. Their past and present. You'd be amazed by our differences and impressed by how we're still so similar.

But languages are taught as if they have nothing to do with history, culture, or mentality. Everything is given as-is with no explanation. Alien words. Confusing rules. Countless exceptions. You'll get exhausted memorizing.

And if you visit the country, you'll realize you can barely speak the language. You can barely attend to the local codes. You can barely get things done. You'll feel you don't know the language at all.

But with all the time and effort required to learn languages, you should be able to reach an understanding with locals. To fit into the local culture. To breeze through the local tasks. That's what it means to learn a language after all.

Studying a language shouldn't be that hard, that long, or that boring. It should be taught through the lens of the native speakers' world. And you should be as excited as if you're reading a page-turner.

It's time to rethink language learning.
